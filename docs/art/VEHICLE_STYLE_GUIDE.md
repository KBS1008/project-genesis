**Project:** Project Genesis
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-12

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official visual language and design standards for all vehicles in Project Genesis.

Vehicles are the visible backbone of logistics. They transport resources, products, passengers and energy throughout the simulated world while providing continuous visual feedback about the state of the economy.

Every vehicle should clearly communicate its purpose, transport capacity and technological level.

# Vehicle Philosophy

Vehicles are functional industrial assets.

Their primary purpose is communication.

A player should immediately recognize:

- transport type
- cargo category
- operating company
- technology level
- operational state

Vehicles should reinforce the impression of a living economy.

---

# Design Goals

Vehicles should be:

- recognizable
- realistic
- modular
- scalable
- readable
- consistent
- animation-friendly

---

# Vehicle Categories

The following categories are planned:

- Road Vehicles
- Rail Vehicles
- Maritime Vehicles
- Aircraft
- Industrial Vehicles
- Construction Vehicles
- Emergency Vehicles
- Utility Vehicles
- Autonomous Vehicles (late game)

Each category should have a distinct visual identity.

---

# Shape Language

Road Vehicles

- compact
- rectangular
- practical

Rail Vehicles

- elongated
- modular
- linear

Ships

- broad
- heavy
- industrial

Aircraft

- aerodynamic
- lightweight
- streamlined

Industrial Vehicles

- robust
- mechanical
- reinforced

Silhouettes should remain recognizable at all zoom levels.

---

# Materials

Preferred materials:

- painted steel
- aluminum
- reinforced plastic
- glass
- rubber
- composite materials

Vehicles should appear well maintained.

Avoid excessive dirt or rust unless representing abandoned assets.

---

# Color Language

Vehicle colors primarily indicate ownership or function.

Examples:

Blue

Company logistics

Green

Agriculture

Yellow

Construction

Orange

Maintenance

White

Research

Red

Emergency services

Neutral gray

Civilian or generic vehicles

Official color values are defined in COLOR_PALETTE.md.

---

# Cargo Visualization

Whenever possible, cargo should be visible.

Examples:

Logs on trucks

Steel coils

Shipping containers

Tank trailers

Grain trailers

Vehicle carriers

Wind turbine blades

Visible cargo strengthens the player's understanding of logistics.

---

# Animation

Vehicles should use subtle animations.

Examples:

- wheel rotation
- track movement
- propeller rotation
- ship wake
- loading cranes
- suspension movement
- warning lights

Animations should support realism rather than spectacle.

---

# Operational States

Vehicles should visually communicate:

- Idle
- Loading
- Unloading
- En Route
- Waiting
- Broken Down
- Under Maintenance

State changes should be easy to identify.

---

# Technological Progression

Vehicles should evolve alongside research.

Typical progression:

Tier I

Mechanical

↓

Tier II

Efficient diesel

↓

Tier III

Electric / Hybrid

↓

Tier IV

Autonomous

↓

Tier V

AI-Optimized Logistics

Visual changes should be evolutionary and believable.

---

# Company Branding

Vehicles may display:

- company logo
- fleet number
- safety markings
- warning labels

Branding should remain subtle.

---

# Scale

Vehicles should remain proportionally correct relative to:

- roads
- buildings
- railways
- airports
- ports
- people

Gameplay readability may justify slight exaggeration.

---

# Environmental Integration

Vehicles should naturally interact with:

- roads
- railways
- intersections
- loading docks
- warehouses
- airports
- harbors

Movement paths should always appear logical.

---

# Accessibility

Vehicles should remain distinguishable through:

- silhouette
- size
- category
- cargo type
- iconography

Never rely solely on color.

---

# AI Asset Generation

AI prompts should define:

- isometric perspective
- industrial realism
- clean geometry
- medium level of detail
- realistic materials
- transparent background
- isolated vehicle
- neutral lighting
- modular construction
- no decorative background
