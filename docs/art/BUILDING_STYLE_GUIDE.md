# Purpose

This document defines the architectural language, visual style and design standards for every building in Project Genesis.

Buildings are among the most important visual assets in the game. They communicate production capabilities, technological progress and economic development while providing immediate visual feedback to the player.

Every building should clearly communicate its function before the player reads its name.

# Building Philosophy

Buildings are functional machines.

Architecture exists to communicate purpose.

Decoration should never hide functionality.

A player should immediately understand:

- what the building does
- which industry it belongs to
- its technological level
- its production capacity
- its operational status

---

# Design Goals

Buildings should be:

- recognizable
- modular
- scalable
- industrial
- believable
- readable
- consistent

---

# Architectural Identity

Project Genesis uses a modern industrial architectural language.

Primary inspiration:

- logistics centers
- automated factories
- research campuses
- renewable energy facilities
- ports
- airports
- industrial parks

Avoid:

- fantasy architecture
- medieval styles
- exaggerated science fiction
- post-apocalyptic environments

---

# Shape Language

Preferred geometry:

- rectangles
- cylinders
- simple roofs
- steel frameworks
- modular halls
- glass facades
- service towers

Complex silhouettes should only exist when they communicate function.

---

# Modular Construction

Every building should appear to be constructed from reusable modules.

Typical modules include:

- production halls
- storage areas
- offices
- loading docks
- utility rooms
- chimneys
- cooling towers
- conveyor connections

This modularity should also support future visual upgrades.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

Every building should contain:

Primary Element

The feature that immediately communicates its purpose.

Secondary Elements

Supporting structures such as tanks, chimneys, silos or cranes.

Tertiary Elements

Small details including pipes, ventilation, signs and lighting.

Players should recognize the primary element first.

---

# Materials

Preferred materials:

- steel
- concrete
- glass
- aluminum
- brick
- asphalt
- solar panels
- vegetation

Materials should appear clean and maintained.

---

# Color Usage

Buildings should primarily use neutral materials.

Functional colors should identify specific systems.

Examples:

Blue

Water

Yellow

Energy

Green

Environmental systems

Orange

Warning areas

Red

Emergency equipment

Color should support understanding rather than decoration.

---

# Roof Design

Roofs should communicate building purpose.

Examples:

Warehouse

Flat roof

Factory

Sawtooth roof

Research Lab

Glass roof

Power Plant

Industrial roof

Farm

Simple pitched roof

Roofs should remain visible in the chosen camera perspective.

---

# Windows

Windows should support realism without dominating the architecture.

Industrial buildings generally have fewer windows than office buildings.

Research facilities may include extensive glass façades.

---

# Entrances

Every building should feature a clearly identifiable entrance.

Possible entrance types:

- personnel entrance
- logistics gate
- loading dock
- maintenance access

Entrances should align with surrounding roads and pathways.

---

# Logistics Integration

Buildings should visually integrate with logistics systems.

Support for:

- roads
- rail
- conveyors
- pipelines
- power lines
- pedestrian access

Connection points should be obvious.

---

# Technological Progression

Buildings should evolve visually with research.

Typical progression:

Level 1

Basic construction

↓

Level 2

Automation added

↓

Level 3

Digital monitoring

↓

Level 4

High-tech industrial campus

Progression should feel evolutionary rather than revolutionary.

---

# Production Feedback

Buildings should visually communicate activity.

Examples:

- moving conveyors
- smoke only when appropriate
- rotating fans
- blinking status lights
- loading vehicles
- animated cranes

Animations should remain subtle.

---

# Environmental Integration

Buildings should fit naturally into their surroundings.

Industrial zones should include:

- roads
- fences
- parking
- vegetation
- utility infrastructure

Avoid isolated floating structures.

---

# Lighting

Lighting should indicate:

- operational status
- nighttime activity
- emergency conditions

Exterior lighting should remain subtle.

---

# Scale

Buildings should follow believable proportions relative to:

- vehicles
- people
- roads
- neighboring structures

Gameplay readability may justify slight exaggeration.

# Damage and Maintenance

Buildings should visually communicate condition.

Possible states:

- Under Construction
- Operational
- Upgrading
- Idle
- Damaged
- Decommissioned

Damage should remain realistic and restrained.

---

# Accessibility

Buildings should remain recognizable:

- at maximum zoom out
- at medium zoom
- at close inspection

Silhouettes are more important than small details.

---

# AI Asset Generation

When generating building concepts with AI, prompts should specify:

- isometric perspective
- modern industrial architecture
- modular construction
- realistic materials
- clean environment
- functional design
- no fantasy elements
- high readability
- neutral background
- consistent lighting

# Summary

Buildings define the visual identity of the economic simulation.

Every building should communicate function, technological progress and industrial purpose through a consistent architectural language based on modularity, realism and clarity.

The objective is to create a believable industrial world that supports strategic gameplay while remaining visually coherent and scalable.
