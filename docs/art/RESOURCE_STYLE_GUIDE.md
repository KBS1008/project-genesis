# Purpose

This document defines the visual standards for all resources in Project Genesis.

Resources are one of the most frequently encountered asset types in the game. They appear in production chains, warehouses, logistics, construction, markets, financial reports and dashboards.

Their visual representation must prioritize instant recognition, consistency and scalability.

# Resource Philosophy

Resources represent materials, products and energy flowing through the economy.

Every resource should communicate:

- what it is
- its material category
- its production stage
- its approximate value
- its relationship to other resources

Recognition should be possible within one second.

---

# Design Goals

Resources should be:

- recognizable
- consistent
- scalable
- visually distinct
- production-oriented
- realistic
- easy to categorize

---

# Categories

Resources belong to one of the following categories:

- Raw Materials
- Agricultural Products
- Industrial Materials
- Manufactured Goods
- Consumer Goods
- Chemicals
- Liquids
- Gases
- Energy
- Waste
- Luxury Goods

Each category should share common visual characteristics.

---

# Shape Language

Each category follows a recognizable shape family.

Examples:

Raw Materials

- irregular
- rough
- natural

Industrial Materials

- geometric
- processed
- standardized

Manufactured Goods

- modular
- packaged
- engineered

Energy

- abstract
- symbolic
- dynamic

Liquids

- containers
- tanks
- barrels
- pipes

Players should recognize the category before identifying the specific resource.

---

# Material Representation

Preferred materials include:

- wood
- stone
- sand
- steel
- aluminum
- concrete
- plastic
- glass
- textiles
- electronics
- food

Surface characteristics should reflect the real-world material while remaining simplified.

---

# Color Language

Color supports categorization.

Suggested mapping:

Brown

Natural resources

Gray

Metals

Blue

Liquids

Green

Agriculture

Yellow

Energy

Black

Oil and coal

White

Processed chemicals

Official values are defined in COLOR_PALETTE.md.

---

# Level of Detail

Resources should contain:

- recognizable silhouette
- simplified surface detail
- limited texture variation

Avoid excessive realism.

---

# Packaging

Processed products may appear as:

- pallets
- crates
- containers
- sacks
- boxes
- barrels
- coils
- bundles

Packaging should communicate logistics readiness.

---

# Scale

Resource icons should remain recognizable at:

- small UI sizes
- inventory views
- warehouse displays
- transport overlays
- production chains

Silhouette is more important than texture.

---

# Production Progression

Whenever applicable, visual progression should reflect industrial refinement.

Example:

Iron Ore

↓

Iron Ingot

↓

Steel Beam

↓

Machine Component

↓

Finished Product

Each stage should clearly communicate increasing processing complexity.

---

# Logistics Representation

Transportable resources should visually indicate their transport form.

Examples:

Coal

Bulk pile

Oil

Tank

Steel

Beams

Wood

Logs

Grain

Sacks or silos

Containerized goods

Shipping container

---

# Storage Representation

Warehouse visualization should remain consistent.

Possible storage forms:

- stacked pallets
- shelves
- silos
- tanks
- bulk piles
- refrigerated storage

---

# Animation

Resources themselves should remain static.

Motion should come from logistics:

- conveyor belts
- trucks
- cranes
- trains
- ships
- robotic arms

---

# AI Asset Generation

AI prompts should define:

- isolated object
- transparent background
- industrial illustration
- isometric lighting
- clean rendering
- medium detail
- realistic material
- consistent visual language
- no text
- no decorative background

---

# Accessibility

Resources should remain distinguishable through:

- silhouette
- shape
- material
- icon

Never rely solely on color.
