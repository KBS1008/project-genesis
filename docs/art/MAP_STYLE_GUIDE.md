**Project:** Project Genesis
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active

# Purpose

This document defines the visual language, environmental design principles and world-building standards for all maps in Project Genesis.

The game world is more than scenery—it is an integral part of the economic simulation. Terrain, infrastructure and natural resources directly influence logistics, production, expansion and strategic planning.

The map should communicate information clearly while remaining visually coherent and immersive.

# Map Philosophy

The map is a living industrial world.

Every landscape element should either:

- influence gameplay,
- support orientation,
- reinforce realism, or
- improve readability.

Decoration should never reduce gameplay clarity.

---

# Design Goals

Maps should be:

- readable
- realistic
- scalable
- modular
- information-rich
- visually calm
- simulation-focused

---

# World Style

Project Genesis represents a believable modern industrial world.

Inspirations include:

- Central Europe
- North America
- Scandinavia
- Modern logistics hubs
- Industrial parks
- Renewable energy landscapes

Avoid:

- fantasy landscapes
- exaggerated biomes
- highly stylized terrain
- unrealistic color saturation

---

# Terrain Types

Supported terrain includes:

- Plains
- Hills
- Mountains
- Forests
- Rivers
- Lakes
- Coastlines
- Wetlands
- Agricultural land
- Urban areas
- Industrial zones

Each terrain type should have a distinct visual identity.

---

# Elevation

Elevation should communicate gameplay.

Higher terrain may influence:

- transport costs
- construction costs
- wind energy
- visibility

Elevation changes should remain readable without becoming visually distracting.

---

# Water

Water bodies should include:

- rivers
- lakes
- reservoirs
- coastlines
- ports

Water should appear calm and readable.

Avoid exaggerated reflections.

---

# Roads

Roads are primary logistics infrastructure.

Road hierarchy:

- Highway
- Primary Road
- Secondary Road
- Local Road
- Service Road

Roads should remain visually distinct at every zoom level.

---

# Railways

Railways should integrate naturally with:

- industry
- ports
- warehouses
- cities

Rail infrastructure should include:

- tracks
- switches
- stations
- freight terminals
- signals

---

# Ports

Ports should contain:

- docks
- cranes
- container terminals
- warehouses
- ship access

Ports should visually communicate heavy logistics activity.

---

# Airports

Airport areas should include:

- runways
- taxiways
- cargo terminals
- hangars
- logistics buildings

Passenger terminals should remain visually secondary unless gameplay requires them.

---

# Vegetation

Vegetation should support realism.

Examples:

- forests
- tree rows
- fields
- bushes
- grass

Vegetation should never obscure important gameplay elements.

---

# Agricultural Areas

Agricultural regions should include:

- crop fields
- orchards
- vineyards
- livestock areas
- irrigation

Field patterns should appear organized and believable.

---

# Industrial Areas

Industrial districts should include:

- factories
- warehouses
- rail access
- roads
- parking
- utility infrastructure

Visual clutter should be minimized.

---

# Urban Areas

Cities should provide context for the economy.

Typical elements:

- residential districts
- commercial buildings
- public services
- parks
- roads

Cities should remain secondary to the player's industrial assets.

---

# Resource Deposits

Natural resources should be identifiable through subtle environmental cues.

Examples:

Iron Ore

Rock formations

Coal

Dark surface deposits

Oil

Pumpjacks and wells

Forests

Dense woodland

Stone

Quarries

Visual hints should support discovery without removing strategic planning.

---

# Environmental Objects

Common map objects include:

- bridges
- tunnels
- fences
- utility poles
- pipelines
- wind turbines
- solar fields
- transmission towers

These should reinforce the industrial atmosphere.

---

# Lighting

Lighting should remain consistent.

Recommended characteristics:

- realistic daylight
- soft shadows
- neutral color temperature
- moderate ambient lighting

Night lighting should emphasize infrastructure rather than decoration.

---

# Weather

Weather should enhance immersion without reducing usability.

Supported conditions:

- clear
- cloudy
- rain
- snow
- fog

Gameplay readability must always take priority.

---

# Seasons

Seasonal changes should affect:

- vegetation
- agriculture
- lighting
- snow coverage
- water appearance

Buildings and roads should remain easily recognizable in every season.

---

# Camera Compatibility

Maps must support:

- strategic zoom
- operational zoom
- inspection zoom

Terrain and infrastructure should remain understandable at every zoom level.

---

# Navigation

Players should always recognize:

- transport corridors
- industrial zones
- resource regions
- expansion opportunities

The map should naturally guide exploration.

---

# Accessibility

Maps should remain readable for:

- color-blind users
- high-contrast mode
- scalable UI

Important gameplay information must never rely solely on color.

---

# AI Environment Generation

AI prompts should specify:

- isometric industrial world
- realistic terrain
- modern infrastructure
- subdued color palette
- clean layout
- readable road network
- believable vegetation
- no fantasy elements
- neutral lighting

# Summary

The world of Project Genesis should function as a readable, believable and strategically meaningful industrial environment.

Every landscape element should support decision-making, logistics and immersion while maintaining a consistent visual language that scales from regional overview to detailed inspection.
