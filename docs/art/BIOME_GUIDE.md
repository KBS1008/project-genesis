**Project:** Project Genesis
**Version:** 1.0

# Purpose

This document defines the official biome system of Project Genesis.

Biomes establish the environmental identity of each map by defining terrain, climate, vegetation, natural resources and infrastructure. They provide visual consistency while supporting meaningful gameplay differences between regions.

Every biome should feel distinct without breaking the overall industrial art direction.

# Biome Philosophy

Biomes are gameplay environments.

They influence:

- resource availability
- transportation
- construction
- agriculture
- energy production
- logistics
- visual identity

A biome should communicate its characteristics immediately through terrain, vegetation and infrastructure.

---

# Design Goals

Biomes should be:

- recognizable
- believable
- strategically meaningful
- visually coherent
- geographically plausible
- scalable

# Resource Distribution

Each biome should provide different strategic opportunities.

Examples:

Temperate Plains

- Agriculture
- Logistics

Forest

- Timber

Mountains

- Metals
- Stone

Coast

- Maritime Trade

Wetlands

- Limited Construction

No biome should provide every important resource.

---

# Infrastructure Style

Infrastructure should adapt naturally.

Examples:

Mountains

- tunnels
- bridges

Coast

- ports
- seawalls

Forest

- logging roads

Agriculture

- rural roads

Industrial

- highways
- rail hubs

---

# Vegetation Principles

Vegetation should:

- reinforce biome identity
- support navigation
- never obscure gameplay

Density should vary naturally.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

Every biome should respond differently to seasons.

Examples:

Winter

- snow
- frozen lakes

Spring

- fresh vegetation

Summer

- dry fields

Autumn

- changing foliage

---

# Weather Integration

Typical weather should vary by biome.

Examples:

Mountains

- snow
- fog

Coast

- storms

- wind

# Construction Rules

Construction suitability differs by biome.

Plains

Excellent

Forest

Moderate

Limited

# Environmental Storytelling

Each biome should communicate:

- history
- economy
- geography
- human activity

Examples include:

- abandoned quarries
- old railway lines
- historical farms
- industrial heritage
- renewable energy projects

Environmental storytelling should remain subtle.

---

# Accessibility

Biomes should remain distinguishable through:

- terrain
- vegetation
- silhouette
- infrastructure

Never rely solely on color.

---

# AI Environment Generation

- realistic geography

- modern infrastructure

- subdued colors
- consistent lighting
- readable landscape

Biomes define the environmental identity of Project Genesis.

Each biome combines geography, climate, natural resources and infrastructure into a coherent gameplay environment. Together they create strategic diversity while maintaining the realistic industrial visual language that characterizes the game.
